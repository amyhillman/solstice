Project Solstice · Design Principles

What the experience
must do.

These are the non-negotiables — the experience commitments that should survive every architectural and product decision made between now and launch.

Core principles
01
Trust is earned at every transition
Every moment a customer moves between products — from BNPL to Card, from Card to Cash Advance — is a trust event, not a navigation event. The experience must acknowledge and honor that. No silent state changes. No invisible eligibility gates.
02
Intent over menu
The Super App is not a product catalog. It routes to the right financial tool based on what the customer is trying to do. "I need $400 this week" is the input. The platform surfaces the best option given their profile, history, and tier. Design for the job, not the product.
03
State is visible and consistent
A customer with a BNPL history, an active Cash Advance, and a Membership tier should experience one coherent Sunbit relationship — not three products sharing a login. Membership tier and product eligibility must say the same thing at the same time.
04
Proactive beats reactive
Don't wait for customers to discover they're eligible for more. The platform should surface relevant next steps — a new product tier, an available loan, a payment insight — at the right moment, with the right framing. The financial partner model is proactive by definition.
05
Built for the Mindful Maximizer
The target customer optimizes across the full credit spectrum. They are not looking for access — they are looking for the best possible outcome. Design that treats them as sophisticated agents, not distressed borrowers. Every interaction should reinforce that Sunbit sees their potential.
06
Architecture must outlast the app
The 2030 platform will launch as a mobile app. The intent layer and customer state must persist beyond it — voice, agents, web, external surfaces. Decisions made now must support a customer relationship that lives past any single channel. That capability cannot be retrofitted.
What this means for NYC

The current app's failure to communicate trust state across products — silent card removals, invisible eligibility changes, no state on re-entry — is not a bug list. It's an architectural signal. The question for the room is not whether we fix the current app. The question is whether any architecture we choose can actually support these principles at scale, and what we don't yet know about our customers that should make us cautious about irreversible decisions.
